Model Bakery’s English Muffins

Biga

  • 1⁄2 cup/75 g bread flour
  • 1⁄4 cup/60 ml water
  • 1⁄4 tsp instant (also called quick-rising or bread machine) yeast

Dough

  • 1 1⁄3 cups/315 ml water
  • 1 3⁄4 tsp instant yeast
  • 1 Tbsp plus 1 tsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 1⁄2 tsp fine sea salt
  • 3 1⁄2 cups/510 g unbleached all-purpose flour, as needed
  • 1⁄4 cup/35g yellow cornmeal, preferably stone-ground
  • 6 Tbsp/90 ml melted Clarified Butter, as needed
  1. TO MAKE THE BIGA:
    • At least 1 day before cooking the muffins, combine the flour, water, and yeast in a small bowl to make a sticky dough.
    • Cover tightly with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 12 hours or up to 24 hours.
  2. TO MAKE THE DOUGH:
    • Combine the biga, water, yeast, olive oil, and salt in the bowl of a stand mixer.
    • Mix on low speed until the mixture looks creamy, about 1 minute.
    • Mix in 3 cups/435 g of the flour to make a soft, sticky dough. Cover with plastic wrap and let stand for 20 minutes.
  3. KNEADING THE DOUGH:
    • Mix in enough of the remaining flour to make a soft dough that barely cleans the mixer bowl.
    • Knead on medium-low speed until smooth and elastic, about 8 minutes.
  4. SHAPING THE DOUGH:
    • Shape the dough into a ball.
    • Oil a medium bowl and place the dough in it, turning to coat with oil.
    • Cover with plastic wrap and let stand in a warm place until doubled in volume, about 2 hours.
  5. RISING THE DOUGH:
    • Scrape the dough onto a floured surface and cut into twelve equal pieces.
    • Shape each into a 4-inch round and coat both sides with cornmeal.
    • Cover loosely with plastic wrap and let rise for about 1 hour.
  6. COOKING THE MUFFINS:
    • Heat clarified butter in a skillet over medium heat.
    • Cook muffins in batches, browning each side for about 6 minutes.
    • Transfer to a paper towel-lined pan and let cool for 20 minutes.
  7. REHEATING THE MUFFINS:
    • Split each muffin in half with a serrated knife.
    • Toast in a broiler or toaster oven until lightly browned. Serve hot.
